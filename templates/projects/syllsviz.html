Syllables

This project deals with the syllable models in Russian and their implementation in Natural Language Processing. There are actually 3 sole Russian models, 2 Western models, at least from western researchers. The app is written in Python.

You can see the results of parsing the 1 million subset of Russian Corpus . Due to the different character of the documents, I took a further subset of 395 documents (from 530 documents) according to the meta-tags

  • "style"=нейтральный/neutral
  • "audience_age"=н-возраст/all
  • "audience_level"=н-уровень/all
  • "subcorpus"=ПК письменных текстов/subcorpus of written texts
There are 5 main models for syllable division
  • 1. Model (SM1) -the Vinogradov model - in Vinogradov, Viktor u. a. (1953). Grammatika russkogo jazyka. Moskva: Academy of Science of the USSR
  • SM2 -the Avanesov model - in Avanesov, Ruben (1956). Fonetika sovremennogo russkogo literaturnogo jazyka. Moskva: Izdat. Moskov. Univ.
  • SM3 -the Bondarko model - in Bondarko (1998). Fonetika sovremennogo russkogo jazyka. St. Petersburg: Izd. S.-Peterburgskogo Univ.
  • SM4 - the Kempgen model- in Kempgen, Sebastian (2003). »Phonologische Silbentrennung im Russischen«. In: Rusistika - Slavistika-Lingvistika. Festschrift für Wemer Lehfeld zum 60. Geburtstag. Hrsg. von Sebastian et al. Kempgen. München: Sagner, S. 195–211 here
  • SM5 -the SSP model - in i.e. Iacoponi, Luca und Renata Savy (2011). »Sylli: Automatic Phonological Syl- labification for Italian.« In: INTERSPEECH. ISCA, S. 641–644. here
