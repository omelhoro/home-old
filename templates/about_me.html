Summary

I'm a former BA-student of Russian Linguistics and History at the U Hamburg. I enjoy combining humanities with digital technologies -> Digital Humanities. My choice of programming language vary with the task: I've solved problems in Scala, Python, Coffeescript, Dart and O'Caml. Although I must say that Python and Coffeescript are my main langs.

Besides programming I like reading science-fiction (especially Stanislaw Lem or short stories) and learning portugues.

I've done projects in various linguistic fields, for example

  • phonological, like automatic syllable dividing, speech synthesis, automatic stress setting
  • morphogical, like tagging, lemmatizing and systemitizing
  • pedagogical, current project
  • vizualization of linguistic phenomena

Take a look at my BA-Thesis:

Vergleich der Silbenmodelle des Russischen mit Hilfe eines Natural Language Processing-Ansatzes

I've built a parser to translate Russian words into syllables according to 5 main models. Then I applied correspondence analysis for finding clusters between the models. An online version of it coming soon.

Or look at my other seminar works.
